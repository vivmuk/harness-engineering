Principles

The harness is the product.

Models change every quarter. A well-built harness outlives them. Three properties make that true.

01

Universal

One recipe grammar for every domain. Legal review, lab automation, game design, incident response. The harness abstracts the domain into tools, state, and stopping conditions, so the same structure ports anywhere.

02

Cost-controlled

Every recipe ships with token budgets, model-tier routing, and hard call ceilings. Agents that cannot overspend by construction. You set the envelope once and the harness enforces it on every run.

03

Self-improving

Harnesses log their own traces and score them against the recipe's evaluation block. Failures become regression cases. The next generation of the recipe is derived from the last generation's evidence.

Framings

Three ways to think about a harness.

Every engineering culture has already solved coordination under constraint. Pick the framing that fits how your team reasons.

Indian

The yantra pattern

A harness as a yantra: a precise geometric instrument where every layer channels intent toward a center. Tools are the outer rings, evaluation is the core, and nothing enters the diagram without a defined place.

Unified

The systems grid

A harness as a control system: inputs, feedback, and a reference signal. Domain-neutral and diagrammatic. This is the default framing the generator uses when you give it nothing but a task description.

Egyptian

The pyramid of scribes

A harness as a hierarchy of scribes: a planner at the apex, workers at the base, and every block placed by rule. Escalation paths are explicit, and the record of construction survives the construction itself.
